How to Differentiate China Chinese & Local Chinese for Dummies

Sep 08 2013
By Hai Lang

Preface

Often times people ask me whether or not a guy/girl comes from China. I was quite annoyed by such questions back then since I thought it's obvious to tell the difference between what I call 'China Chinese' and 'Local Chinese'.

I realized that I was wrong when I failed to differentiate 'India Indians' and 'Local Indians', and my friend Kumar can judge even by the way how they walk. I started to get the hang of it after he explained me some tricks, and then I thought, hey, maybe I can do everyone a favor and share some basic tips on how to do the same trick on my beloved Chinese fellows ;-)

About This Guide

If you are about to go after some Chinese guy/girl or just want to expand your social circle to have more Chinese friends, and you are troubled by the difference between 'China Chinese' and 'Local Chinese', but you have none or minimal knowledge of any kind of Chinese culture, and you don't speak Mandarin, Cantonese, or Hokkien, then no worries, this guide is for you.

Technical Stuff (You can skip this safely)

For starters, most local Chinese originated from southern part of China, namely Guangdong and Fujian province. The dialects they speak are Cantonese and Hokkien accordingly.

As shown in the map above, including Hainan island, the last three rows on the map are normally considered as the 'Southern China'. All Chinese folks share the same written language, but the difference between dialects, esp. southern vs northern are often overwhelming even to a native Chinese speaker.

We share roughly the same culture, with slight differences between provinces. Knowing some of the differences can even help to tell which province a person comes from. It becomes rather obvious when it comes to southern vs. northern. For instance, people are still arguing whether Zongzi should be salty or sweetened throughout different parts of China. (I'm team sweet by the way.)

As what I said, most 'Local Chinese''s forefathers were born and rised in Guangdong, Fujian, and in some cases Hainan, hijacked by our beloved British, and travelled all the way to Malaysia / Indonesia / America and so on. Although generations of living in another country has developped them some strange (no offence) habbits, they still carry some rather distinct and obvious culture heritages from their home provinces.

On the other hand, most 'China Chinese' you meet nowadays are highly unlikely from Guangdong or Fujian (Look at the sizes of all other provinces and imagine the population there). And even if they do come from the said provinces, Mandarin is now the official language we speak throughout China, and that greatly affects our English accents (I'll talk about it later). And this is the foundation of our theory, knowing the differences between these two provinces and all others would be sufficient to master the art of differentiating Chinese folks.

Here Comes the Actual Guide

DISCLAIMER: This guide is just for fun. Appologize if I offended anyone in anyway

A. The way we speak English

  1. If a person speaks English with a trailing 'Lah', 'Mah', 'Loh' etc. He/She is a Local.
  2. If a person speaks English with the Russel Peter's classic Chinese accent. He/She is a Local.
  3. If a person speaks English like 'chopping vegetables' and trying so hard on every pronunciation. He/She comes from China.
  4. China Chinese pronunce 'head' as 'hand', 'world' as 'word', etc. Anything involving tongue-twisting is mission impossible to us.
  1. Local Chinese generally speaks better English.
  2. But ironically, people from China pay more attention to grammars even we sometimes can't handle the pronunciation.

B. The way we dress

  1. If a person wears sunglasses. He/She is probably from China.
  1. If a person wears shorts/slippers. He/She is probably a Local.
  2. Similarly (esp for guys) if you've never seen a person wearing shorts/slippers. He/She is from China.
  3. If a person dresses with an overwhelmed sense of 'fashion'. He/She is from China.
  4. Similarly, if a person has no sense of fashion whatsoever. He/She is a Local.
  5. If you see a guy with a huge water bottle all the time. He/She is definitely a Local.

C. How our names look like

  1. If a you can't comfortablly pronunce a person's name. He/She is from China. (My name is pronunced as High Long, and yes I freaking love it)
  2. If you can easily pronunce a person's name, but it made you laugh. He/She is Local.
  3. We China Chinese don't usually have christian names. Except for when we call Dominos.

D. The way we drive

  1. If a person horns a lot while driving. He/She is from China.
  2. If a person curses a lot while driving. He/She is from China.
  3. If a person drives in a great haste as if someone's going to deliver a baby. He/She is from China.
  4. If you find a Chinese guy in a car accident scene. He/She is Local (just kidding.)

E. The way we drink/party/clubbing

  1. If you've never seen a person drunk. He/She is from China.
  2. If a person leaves in the middle to 'send gf/bf home'. He/She is from China.
  3. If a person shouts like a Russian after getting drunk. He/She is from China.
  4. If a person drinks a lot but never dance. He/She is from China.
  5. If a person dances a lot but can't drink. He/She is Local.
  6. If a person drinks in a way as if he/she's trying to close a deal with you. He/She is from China.
  1. If a girl orders bloody mary. She's from China.
  2. If a guy orders bloody mary. He's a Local. (Sorry...)

F. The way we text

  1. If a person uses 'XD', 'LMAO', 'OMFG', 'IDK' a lot. He/She is a Local.
  2. If a person uses '^_^', '^ω^'', 'T_T', '-_-', 'LOL', 'hehe', a lot. He/She is from China.
  1. If a person always put '~' at the end of each sentence. He/She is from China.
  2. If a person uses extensive amount of '.....'. He/She is from China

G. The way we curse/fight

  1. If a person curses in a funny way. He/She is a Local.
  2. If a person never curse. He/She is from China. (Our English proficiency doesn't allow us to curse effectively.)
  3. If a person threats to call his/her gang. He/She is from China.
  1. If a person fights in a boxing style. He/She is a Local.

H. The way we eat

  1. People obsessed with Pizza/McD/KFC are most likely from China.
  2. People obsessed with instant noodles are most likely from China.
  3. People obsessed with spicy food are most likely from China.
  4. If someone knows how to actually make dumplings, He/She's from China.
  5. If someone looks angry having so-called 'Chinese food', He/She's from China.
  6. If someone looks grumpy having Chinese food, He/She's a Local.
  7. A person who asks for chopstick when having Pizzahut / Thai Food is a Local.
  8. A person who insists on using dining knife and forks comes from China.

I. The way we shop

  1. If a person (esp. girls) is obsessed with luxury brands. He/She is from China.
  2. If a preson knows many brands but failed to pronunce their names. He/She is from China.
  3. If a person looks like He/She owns the place, and is about to fire everyone while walking into a luxury brand outlet. He/She is from China.
  4. If you see someone buying large amount of bags or cosmetics. He/She is from China, buying gifts for relatives and friends.

Conclusion

I'll just stop here. This is no way near a Definitive Guide, I just listed some of the obvious/funny/interesting differences. And you are welcome to add more to the comment section below.

Appologize to all my Chinese fellows and friends, no offence intended. I love you all! ;)
